What Is a Website Roast?

A website roast is a practical review of your site from the perspective of trust, clarity, user experience, and lead generation. I'll point out what's working, what feels weak, and where your site may be losing attention, confidence, or conversions.

First Impression

Does your site immediately look trustworthy and relevant?

Clarity

Is it obvious what you do, who you help, and what visitors should do next?

Conversion

Are your calls to action, layout, and messaging helping visitors take action?

What I Review

Headline clarity

Does your main headline quickly communicate your offer or value?

Visual trust and professionalism

Does the design feel credible and aligned with your brand?

Calls to action

Are your buttons and links clear, visible, and compelling?

Mobile friendliness

Does your site work well and look good on phones?

Layout and content hierarchy

Can visitors scan and find what matters without getting lost?

Service clarity

Is it obvious what you offer and who it's for?

Lead flow / conversion path

Is the journey from visitor to lead or customer clear?

Contact experience

How easy and inviting is it for visitors to reach you?

Messaging strength

Does your copy speak directly to your ideal customers?

Overall first impression

What does a new visitor think in the first 3 seconds?

Small changes in structure, messaging, and call-to-action placement can make a big difference in how your website performs.

Why This Matters

A website doesn't need to be flashy to perform well — it needs to be clear, trustworthy, and built to guide people toward action.
