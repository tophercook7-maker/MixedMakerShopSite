Example findings (from real roasts)

The kind of feedback that shows up in a typical roast — actionable, specific, and free of jargon.

"Your phone number isn\'t tappable on mobile."

Half your visitors are on a phone. If your number is text instead of a tap-to-call link, you\'re losing calls you don\'t even know about. One CSS tweak fixes it.

"Your homepage doesn\'t say what you do in the first sentence."

Visitors decide whether you\'re relevant in under 5 seconds. Your headline says "We deliver excellence" — which means nothing. Replace with "Lawn care in Hot Springs, AR" and watch bounce rate drop.

"Your contact form is buried three pages deep."

Customers who want to call you have to: home → services → contact → form. Each click costs 30-50% of them. Put the form (or a clear CTA) on every page.

"Your hours haven\'t been updated since 2022."

If a visitor sees outdated info, they assume the business might not be open. This single fix often recovers more leads than a $5,000 redesign.

FAQ

What does the website roast actually include?

A short personally-written review (3-7 specific findings) covering trust signals, clarity, calls-to-action, mobile experience, and lead-flow path. We focus on the highest-impact issues — the ones costing you visitors right now — instead of an exhaustive 50-item checklist nobody reads.

Is it really free? What\'s the catch?

Yes, free. No obligation, no credit card, no scheduled sales call. The catch is small: we mention the relevant MixedMakerShop service (web design, local SEO, redesign) at the end so you know what fixing it would look like. If you want help, great. If you just want the feedback, that\'s also great.

How long does it take to receive the roast?

Usually 24-48 hours after you submit. Topher reviews each one personally, not a script. Busy weeks might push it to 3 business days but we always reply with at least a status update.

Will you publicly post my site?

No. The roast is private — sent to your email only. We never share, post, or critique your site publicly. The whole point is honest feedback you can actually use without your customers seeing it.

What if I disagree with the feedback?

Push back. Most of the value comes from the conversation that starts with the roast, not the bullet points themselves. If a finding doesn\'t apply to your business, tell us why — we\'ll usually find something better to focus on.

Do you roast websites outside Hot Springs?

Yes. We\'re based in Hot Springs, AR, but the roast is entirely remote. Any small business website anywhere is fair game. The local SEO commentary is sharper for Arkansas businesses, but the structural feedback applies everywhere.
