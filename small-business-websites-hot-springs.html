Why local-only matters

If you sell to customers in Hot Springs, your competition isn't national agencies — it's the other Hot Springs business down the street. That changes everything about how the website should be built.

A national web designer builds for a national audience. They don't know that "Park Avenue" in Hot Springs is different from "Park Avenue" in New York. They don't know which neighborhoods you serve, what the locals call landmarks, or what time the high school football game runs.

Local web design is meaningfully different. We mention Bathhouse Row, Lake Hamilton, Hot Springs Village, Mid-America Science Museum — because that's the geography customers actually live in. We tune Google Business Profile categories for Hot Springs-specific searches. We know that "near me" in Hot Springs means a different radius than "near me" in Little Rock.

The work isn't harder. It's just specific. Done right, it ranks for the searches that actually matter to you.
