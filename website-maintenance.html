Maintenance is what makes a website earn its keep

A new website is exciting. The build feels productive, the launch feels like an accomplishment, and then it sits there. Within a year, the hours are slightly wrong, the photos are slightly old, plugins are out of date, and Google is starting to notice.

Within two years it's embarrassing to send people to. By year three it's actively losing you customers and you're shopping for a redesign you didn't need to buy.

Maintenance is what prevents that cycle. A small monthly investment in keeping the site current is dramatically cheaper than rebuilding every three years. And the side effect — knowing someone is actually watching the site — catches small problems before they become real ones.

Frequently asked questions

How fast do edits get done?

Small edits (hours, phone, photo swap, new testimonial) are usually same-day or next-day. Larger changes (new pages, redesigned sections) get a quick scope-and-quote first and typically ship within the week.

Do you maintain websites you didn't build?

Sometimes. We'll take a look first. If the site is on a platform we know (WordPress, Wix, Squarespace, Shopify, Webflow, static HTML) and isn't a custom-built disaster underneath, we'll maintain it. If it's a tangle we can't safely touch, we'll be straight with you.

What if I want to cancel?

Month-to-month. Cancel any time. You keep all your files, your hosting, your domain — everything stays with you.

Do you also fix things I broke trying to edit it myself?

Yes, this is half of what maintenance ends up being. Don't feel bad — text us a screenshot.

Can I have multiple sites under one maintenance plan?

Yes. Each additional site is added at a small per-site fee. Useful if you run multiple brands or locations on separate domains.
